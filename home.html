What I've Done

Program Management - Microsoft

During the summer of 2016, I interned at Microsoft as a program manager on the Instrumentation, Diagnostics, and Telemetry team working with enterprises on creating a tool to help them understand telemetry.

HoloLens

During the 2016 Microsoft //oneweek hackathon, my friends and I created a minigolf game in Unity for the HoloLens. I loved learning an exciting, new technology and the intensity of work and pressure during this period.

16-Bit Processor

Using Cadence and Verilog, I created and simulated a 16-bit processor that incorporated datapath, control, register file, and I/O for my VHDL class. It came with 6 assembly-level instructions.

MorseKey, MacroKeys

Using Arduino, I experimented with very simple interfaces that still provided users with a lot of power. One was a single-button Morse keyboard and the other a customizable macro keypad.

Nichrome Icicle Cutter

From September - December 2016, my friends and I developed an icicle cutting machine that ended up in international news. Our startup made it into the Harvard Innovation Lab.

Agabros

Over the summmer of 2015, I developed a versatile product with Seth Donoughe from the Extavour lab at Harvard. One of its uses allows many miniscule samples to be imaged at once.

Experimental Cochlear Implant

During the summer of 2014, I worked on a tool for experimental fully implantable cochlear implants with researchers from Massachusetts Eye and Ear Infirmary and NYU Langone Medical Center.

Cochlear Implant Simulation

I made a cochlear implant impulse simulator during the summer of 2013 that simulated experimental stimulation patterns before trying them on current cochlear implant users.

NASA JPL

During the summer of 2012, I had the privilege of being an employee at the Jet Propulsion Lab where I created a radiation model for the magnetic atmosphere of Uranus.
